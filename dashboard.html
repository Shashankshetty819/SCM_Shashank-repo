You've got Meds to take

Diuretics

For ascites and edema. Furosemide: Typically initiated at 20 mg orally once daily, with adjustments based on response and electrolyte levels.

Ursodeoxycholic Acid (UDCA)

For liver function. Ursodeoxycholic acid is commonly prescribed at a dose of 13 mg/kg/day orally, typically divided into two or three doses.

Beta-blocker

For variceal bleeding prophylaxis. Propranolol or nadolol may be initiated at low doses and titrated upwards to achieve a target heart rate reduction of 25%.

Vitamin K

For coagulopathy. Supplementation is typically administered orally or parenterally, with dosages varying based on laboratory monitoring.
